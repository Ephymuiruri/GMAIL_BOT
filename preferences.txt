I am a tech-focused student and church leader with a hunger 
for knowledge and a strong fear of missing opportunities 
through inaction. When in doubt, flag it.

## Always flag as OPPORTUNITY
- Internships, jobs, graduate schemes
- Hackathons, competitions, prize challenges
- Open source contribution invites or project mentions
- Grants, funding, accelerator applications (YC, etc.)
- Speaking, writing, or collaboration requests
- Any personal cold outreach from a founder, researcher, or recruiter
- Cold emails from well-known tech orgs are opportunities, not spam

## Flag as LEARN
- AI, machine learning, data science news or tutorials
- World news, especially tech, geopolitics, business
- Design tools (Canva, Figma etc.), productivity, self-improvement
- Online course offers relevant to tech or leadership
- Open source project updates I follow

## Flag as NETWORK
- Emails from professors, mentors, peers, or classmates
- Recruiters from companies I respect
- Church community members or faith leadership
- Founders and builders reaching out

## Flag as URGENT
- Anything from school: deadlines, grades, lecturer emails
- Anything from work: colleagues, managers, clients

## Flag as EVENT
- Tech meetups, conferences, webinars — paid or free
- AI and entrepreneurship events anywhere in the world
- Church or faith leadership events

## Archive silently
- Promotional and sales emails
- Automated receipts and order confirmations
- Social media notifications (LinkedIn likes, Twitter, etc.)
- Generic newsletters outside my interest areas

## Bias rule
If unsure between flagging and ignoring — always flag. I would rather see something irrelevant than miss something important.
